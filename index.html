Moonshots · Presenting Novara · 2025

As a fellow at The Knowledge Society (TKS), I've spent this year going deep on one of the hardest problems in biomedicine: the foreign body response. When a medical device is implanted in the human body, the immune system fights it, often causing the very device meant to save a life to fail. I set out to understand why.

Through four intensive phases: Rep 1, Rep 2, Review, and Create, I mapped the inflammatory cascade, fibrotic encapsulation, and what materials science and engineering can do about it. That research became the foundation for Novara.

At TKS Moonshots, I co-presented Novara: a microneedle-based device designed to detect sepsis early. Sepsis kills millions because detection comes too late. Novara changes that.

Gold Medal — Diefenbaker Speech Tournament

Junior Newbie Impromptu Speaking · 2025

Impromptu speaking is one of the most revealing formats in competitive speech. You receive a prompt, and seconds to think. No script. No notes. Just your mind, your voice, and the room.

Winning gold at the Diefenbaker Tournament isn't something I prepared for in the traditional sense. It reflects how I actually think, how I find structure in chaos, make a point land, and connect with an audience on the fly.

Public speaking is where TKS, research, and community work all converge. Every time I present Novara or facilitate a discussion at CCASA, I'm doing the same thing: making something complex feel accessible.
